About NeuroTech Robotics

Club introduction

NeuroTech Robotics is a student‑led club that brings together enthusiastic engineers to learn and build across diverse robotics fields: industrial, agricultural, medicinal, service and educational robotics. The club provides a hands‑on platform where students design prototypes, experiment with emerging technologies, and apply engineering principles to solve real‑world challenges.

Goals & objectives

  • Empower students aged 16+ to explore robotics and STEM careers.
  • Provide hands‑on experience in coding, electronics and robot design.
  • Bridge the gap between Botswana and technology by positioning students as contributors to national digital transformation.
  • Support community development through applied robotics projects in agriculture, healthcare and education.

MORE ABOUT US:

Membership & eligibility

Eligibility: Students aged 16 and older who are residents of Botswana with a minimum Junior Certificate and prior computer studies are eligible. Applicants aged 16–17 must register with a guardian. Membership is open to senior secondary and tertiary learners.

Information architecture & taxonomy

The club and website are organized by five robotics categories so students can choose a track and find relevant projects, events and learning resources quickly.

CATEGORIES INCLUDE:

Industrial Robotics

Focus: automation, PLC basics, robotic arms, vision systems and pick‑and‑place prototypes.
Why it matters: industrial robotics skills prepare students for manufacturing automation, maintenance and systems integration roles as local industry modernizes.
Project examples: low‑cost SCARA arm, conveyor sorter, vision‑guided pick‑and‑place.

Agricultural Robotics

Focus: precision sensing, autonomous rovers, drone payloads and irrigation controllers.
Why it matters: Botswana's agricultural sector benefits from low‑cost sensing and automation to increase yields and reduce resource use.
Project examples: crop‑monitor rover, automated irrigation controller, drone NDVI payload.

Medicinal Robotics

Focus: assistive devices, telepresence for clinics, rehabilitation aids and low‑cost diagnostic supports.
Why it matters: robotics can extend healthcare reach in remote areas and support clinic workflows.
Project examples: telepresence cart, medication reminder robot, hand‑rehab device.

Service Robotics

Focus: delivery robots, campus assistants, human‑robot interaction and UX design.
Why it matters: service robots improve logistics and accessibility in campuses and public spaces.
Project examples: indoor delivery bot, voice‑enabled help kiosk, outreach assistant.

Educational Robotics

Focus: modular kits, curricula and outreach to teach coding, electronics and systems thinking.
Why it matters: building a pipeline of STEM talent through hands‑on learning and teacher support.
Project examples: modular STEM kits, classroom challenges, teacher training modules.
