About NeuroTech Robotics

Club introduction

NeuroTech Robotics is a student‑led club dedicated to bridging the gap between Botswana and emerging technologies through hands‑on exploration of robotics. Founded to empower young engineers and innovators, the club provides a collaborative platform where members design prototypes, experiment with cutting‑edge tools, and apply engineering principles to solve real‑world challenges. By engaging in diverse fields such as industrial, agricultural, medicinal, service, and educational robotics, NeuroTech Robotics nurtures creativity, teamwork, and technical excellence while preparing students to contribute meaningfully to the country’s digital transformation.

Our identity is shaped by five core values: Impact, ensuring our projects create meaningful change in communities; Teamwork, fostering collaboration and shared learning; Fun, making robotics an enjoyable and inspiring journey; Innovation, driving new ideas and solutions; and Discovery, encouraging curiosity and exploration of technology’s possibilities. Guided by these values and our slogan “Wired by Innovation. Driven by Purpose,” NeuroTech Robotics empowers students to gain practical skills in coding, electronics, and robot design, positioning them as future leaders in Botswana’s growing STEM ecosystem and beyond.

Goals & objectives

  • Empower students aged 16+ to explore robotics and STEM careers.
  • Provide hands‑on experience in coding, electronics and robot design.
  • Bridge the gap between Botswana and technology by positioning students as contributors to national digital transformation.
  • Support community development through applied robotics projects in agriculture, healthcare and education.

MORE ABOUT US:

NeuroTech Robotics was founded by a former robotics student who envisioned a space where young innovators in Botswana could explore technology beyond the classroom. What began as a small initiative has grown into a vibrant student‑led club that brings together learners passionate about coding, electronics, and robot design. Rooted in the belief that hands‑on experience is the best teacher, NeuroTech Robotics provides members with opportunities to prototype, experiment, and apply engineering principles to real‑world challenges.

Our journey is guided by clear objectives: to inspire students through workshops and competitions, to foster collaboration across disciplines, and to create solutions that make a tangible impact in communities. From agricultural robots that support local farming to healthcare assistants that demonstrate the potential of robotics in medicine, our projects reflect both creativity and responsibility.

Looking ahead, NeuroTech Robotics aims to expand into areas such as artificial intelligence and industrial automation, positioning itself as a leading hub for innovation in Botswana. By staying true to our core values of Impact, Teamwork, Fun, Innovation, and Discovery, we continue to empower students to become future leaders in STEM and active contributors to the country’s digital transformation.

Membership & eligibility

Eligibility: Students aged 16 and older who are residents of Botswana with a minimum Junior Certificate and prior computer studies are eligible. Applicants aged 16–17 must register with a guardian. Membership is open to senior secondary and tertiary learners.

Information architecture & taxonomy

The club and website are organized by five robotics categories so students can choose a track and find relevant projects, events and learning resources quickly.

CATEGORIES INCLUDE:

Industrial Robotics

Focus: automation, PLC basics, robotic arms, vision systems and pick‑and‑place prototypes.
Why it matters: industrial robotics skills prepare students for manufacturing automation, maintenance and systems integration roles as local industry modernizes.
Project examples: low‑cost SCARA arm, conveyor sorter, vision‑guided pick‑and‑place.

Agricultural Robotics

Focus: precision sensing, autonomous rovers, drone payloads and irrigation controllers.
Why it matters: Botswana's agricultural sector benefits from low‑cost sensing and automation to increase yields and reduce resource use.
Project examples: crop‑monitor rover, automated irrigation controller, drone NDVI payload.

Medicinal Robotics

Focus: assistive devices, telepresence for clinics, rehabilitation aids and low‑cost diagnostic supports.
Why it matters: robotics can extend healthcare reach in remote areas and support clinic workflows.
Project examples: telepresence cart, medication reminder robot, hand‑rehab device.

Service Robotics

Focus: delivery robots, campus assistants, human‑robot interaction and UX design.
Why it matters: service robots improve logistics and accessibility in campuses and public spaces.
Project examples: indoor delivery bot, voice‑enabled help kiosk, outreach assistant.

Educational Robotics

Focus: modular kits, curricula and outreach to teach coding, electronics and systems thinking.
Why it matters: building a pipeline of STEM talent through hands‑on learning and teacher support.
Project examples: modular STEM kits, classroom challenges, teacher training modules.
